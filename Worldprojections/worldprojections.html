In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

The first thing I did in order to display the projections on QGIS was enable the indicatrix mapper, which plots circles of equal size on the projected map of choice in order to show distortion. To choose a projection on QGIS, I clicked the button in the bottom right corner of a new project on QGIS that allowed me to alter the CRS.

WGS84 Projection

This projection preserves direction as shown by the perfectly straight grid. Though it preserves shape and size fairly well along the equator, as one nears the poles, the size and shape of the world are distorted.

Aitoff Projection

The Aitoff projection distorts size, shape, and direction- it is not on a straight grid and the size and shape of the indicatrix vary greatly as one moves away from the Prime Meridian.

World Pseudo Mercator Projection

The Pseudo Mercator projection preserves shape and direction as shown by the grid and the uniform shape of the circles. However, size is greatly distorted as one approaches the poles, as shown by the circles by the poles that seem at least double the size of the circles along the equator.

World Winkel I Projection

The Winkel I projection appears to minimally distort size, but distorts direction as shown by the bent lines making up the grid. Also distorted is the shape of parts of the world, evident from the variance of the shapes of the circles as one moves towards the poles.

World Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

This projection distorts shape, size, and direction greatly (except for direction from the north pole directly south). Shape and size are both highly distorted.

Equal Area Cylindrical Projection

The Equal Area Cylindrical Projection maintains size and direction but not shape. The grid aligning the circles is linear but the shape changes drastically as one moves away from the equator. The area of the circles, however, is consistent.

Equidistant Conic Projection

The equidistant conic projection has similar distortion of size, shape, and direction to the Azimuthal Equidistantprojection, but distorts the shape of the circles less.

World Wagner IV Projection

The Wagner IV map appears very similar to the Winkel I projection- just a bit more stretched out, which makes me think that it has a greater distortion of distance. Shape and direction are distorted- even along the equator. However, size appears to remain fairly consistent between the circles.

World Van Der Grinten I Projection

This projection distorts size, shape, and direction- it is in an interesting shape that gives it a bent grid and thus distorted direction. The land masses by the poles also appear much larger as demonstrated by the large circles in those areas.
